United State's Economy

The USA has a highly advanced high-income market economy. It is the world's largest economy by nominal GDP and net-worth. It has the most technologically powerful and innovative economy in the world. The USA is the world's largest importer and second largest exporter. The abundance of natural resources, a well-developed infrastructure and high productivity fuels the nation's economy.

Time zone difference

The USA is divided into nine standard time zones covering the states and territories. Most of the states observe Daylight Saving time for approximately the spring, summer and fall months. As defined by law, the different time zones are as follows- AST- Atlantic Standard Time (UTC+4:00), EST- Eastern Standard Time (UTC+5:00), CST- Central Standard Time (UTC+6:00), MST- Mountain Standard Time (UTC+7:00), PST- Pacific Standard Time (UTC+8:00), AKST- Alaskan Standard Time (UTC+9:00), HST- Hawaii Aleutian Standard Time (UTC+10:00), UTC-11 Samoa Standard Time (UTC+11:00), Chamorro Standard Time (UTC+12:00).

Weather in United States

Unites State's Weather

Earth’s most diverse weather system.

The U.S. is a country with huge landmass and has a continental climate in general. One can experience different climatic conditions such as oceanic, tropical, subtropical, humid temperate, warm-hot desert, semi-arid and humid continental. The weather varies from region to region, for example winters in Utah and Wyoming can be crisp and frigid. Summers in southern parts of the country can be hot and humid. The western cities of the continent can have extremely cold winters with limited sunshine.
